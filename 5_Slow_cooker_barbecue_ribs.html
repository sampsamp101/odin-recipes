Slow-Cooker Barbecue Ribs

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds lb pork baby back ribs
  • 1 teaspoons salt
  • 0.5 teaspoons black pepper
  • 1 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoons onion powder
  • 1 teaspoons smoked paprika
  • 0.5 small onion, sliced
  • 2 cups BBQ sauce
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce

Steps

  1. Prep the ribs: Remove the thin membrane from the back of 3 pounds lb pork baby back ribs if still attached, then cut the racks into sections that will fit in your slow cooker.
  2. Season the ribs: In a small bowl, mix together 1 teaspoons salt, 0.5 teaspoons black pepper, 1 teaspoons garlic powder, 1 teaspoons onion powder, and 1 teaspoons smoked paprika. Rub this seasoning mixture all over the ribs.
  3. Layer in slow cooker: Place 0.5 small onion, sliced in the bottom of the slow cooker, then arrange the seasoned ribs on top, standing them on their sides around the edge if needed to fit.
  4. Make and add the sauce: In a bowl, whisk together 2 cups BBQ sauce, 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar, and 1 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce. Pour about two-thirds of this sauce over the ribs, reserving the rest for later.
  5. Slow cook: Cover and cook on low until the ribs are tender and pulling away from the bone.
  6. Glaze and serve: Carefully transfer the ribs to a platter, brush with the reserved sauce, and serve warm.
