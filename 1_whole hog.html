Carolina-Style Whole Hog Barbecue

Ingredients

The Hog

  • 1 whole hog (80–120 lbs), cleaned and butterflied

Dry Rub

  • ½ cup kosher salt
  • ½ cup black pepper
  • ¼ cup red pepper flakes
  • ¼ cup paprika
  • 2 tbsp garlic powder
  • 2 tbsp onion powder
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar

Eastern Carolina Vinegar Sauce (for basting & serving)

  • 4 cups apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tbsp red pepper flakes
  • 1 tbsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 tbsp sugar (optional)

Wood (for smoking)

  • Hickory or oak wood — enough for 12–16 hours of smoke

Steps

  1. Mix all dry rub ingredients together in a bowl
  2. Pat the hog dry and apply the rub generously inside the cavity and over the skin
  3. Let the hog rest at room temperature for 1 hour before cooking
  4. Prepare your pit or smoker — target a temperature of 225–250°F (107–121°C)
  5. Place the hog skin-side up over indirect heat
  6. Maintain pit temperature and add wood every 45–60 minutes to keep smoke going
  7. Mix all vinegar sauce ingredients together and let sit at least 30 minutes
  8. After 6 hours, flip the hog skin-side down
  9. Baste the meat with vinegar sauce every hour
  10. Cook for a total of 12–16 hours until internal temperature reaches 195°F (90°C)
  11. Flip skin-side up for the last 30 minutes to crisp the skin
  12. Remove from pit and let rest 30 minutes
  13. Pull and chop the pork, mixing in skin pieces
  14. Toss with additional vinegar sauce to taste and serve
